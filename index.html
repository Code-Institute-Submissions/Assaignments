Amur Leopard Conservation

The Amur Leopard is considered one of the most endagered species to date, with only around 84 individuals in the wild. It therefore needs our help to rebuild its population. The underlying cause of the small population is there low genetic variation, which can cause complications in breeding process. Besides this many external influences has cause the decline of the breed, such as: Poaching, Human Interaction and Habitat Loss. These can all be attributed to humans and therfore its up to us to make the changes to not lot let this creature go extinct

Habitat Loss

Arguably the biggest factor affecting the Amur Leopard is the destruction of their habitat. The Amur Leopard is situated in one area; the Primorye region of southeastern Russia and northern China. Between 1970 and 1983, 80 percent of the Amur leopard's habitat was lost due to logging, forest fires, and agricultural land conversion projects. This no only destroys the homes of the leopard, but also the prey that it feed on, which is mainly deer, but also includes roe deer, sika deer, badgers and hares. Pressure on gouvernements and local authorities is needed to decrease deforestation and habitat loss.

Poaching

Another key factor killing the Leopard is Poaching. Amur Leopards are killed fro their beautiful, dotted, fur skins and sold on the black market for a high price. In 14 months from February 2002 to April 2003, seven skins or part of skins were confiscated, six in Russia and one in China. The Amur Leopard is also used in some Chinese medicines, with their bones, when crushed up, is said to heal certain ailments. These practices are banned but not enforced by the Gouvernmental bodies in the area.
